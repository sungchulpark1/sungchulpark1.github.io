About Me

I was born in Gwangju, South Korea and moved to Ontario, Canada at the age of 3, where I've lived ever since.

My journey with computers began early. I was on a PC by age 3, playing Diablo I and StarCraft I by age 5. By 7, I was exploring StarCraft’s map editor, and by 9, I was diving into advanced scripting through Warcraft III's World Editor.

At 12, I built and hosted a private MMORPG server that attracted over 150 daily active players for several months. That experience introduced me to networking, server management, and a passionate community.

In my teens, I started using Game Maker to bring my own ideas to life. I collaborated with others on small game projects and by 16, I launched an online multiplayer game that reached 50+ daily players and fostered a tight-knit community.

At 18, I pursued formal training in game development at Seneca College, studying game programming and design to sharpen my skills and turn my passion into a career.

Over the years, I’ve transitioned from game development to full stack web development, applying my programming skills to create engaging web applications. My background in game design has given me a unique perspective on user experience and interactivity, which I bring to every project I work on.

Today, I’m a full stack developer with a focus on creating engaging and interactive web applications. My journey has been driven by a love for technology and a desire to connect with others through the games and applications I build.
